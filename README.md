# British Airways Booking Behaviour Analysis

## Project Overview

This project analyses customer booking behaviour using a British Airways booking dataset. The objective was to identify factors associated with booking completion and understand how customer preferences, booking channels, travel characteristics, and ancillary services influence conversion outcomes.

---

## Business Problem

Airlines experience significant booking abandonment during the customer journey. Understanding which factors are associated with completed bookings can help improve conversion rates, optimise the booking experience, and increase revenue.

---

## Dataset

The dataset contains customer booking records including:

* Number of passengers
* Sales channel
* Trip type
* Purchase lead time
* Length of stay
* Flight hour
* Flight day
* Route
* Booking origin
* Extra baggage selection
* Preferred seat selection
* In-flight meal selection
* Flight duration
* Booking completion status

---

## Data Preparation

* Reviewed and validated the dataset
* Removed 719 duplicate records
* Final dataset contained 49,282 records
* No missing values identified

## Key Findings

### Booking Performance

* Overall booking completion rate: **15.0%**
* Overall booking abandonment rate: **85.0%**

### Sales Channel Performance

* Internet users achieved a higher booking completion rate (**15.5%**) than mobile users (**11.0%**)
* Mobile bookings represent a significant opportunity for optimisation

### Ancillary Service Behaviour

Customers purchasing ancillary services demonstrated higher booking completion rates:

| Service         | Not Selected | Selected |
| --------------- | ------------ | -------- |
| Extra Baggage   | 11.5%        | 16.7%    |
| Preferred Seat  | 13.8%        | 17.8%    |
| In-Flight Meals | 14.2%        | 16.1%    |

### Customer Intent

Customers selecting additional services appeared more committed to completing their booking journey, suggesting ancillary purchases are strong indicators of booking intent.

---

## Business Recommendations

1. Improve the mobile booking experience to reduce customer abandonment.
2. Promote ancillary services throughout the booking journey.
3. Investigate customer drop-off points within the booking funnel.
4. Use customer preference data to personalise offers and improve conversion.
5. Target high-intent customers with tailored ancillary service bundles.

---

## Project Outcomes

This analysis successfully identified several factors associated with booking completion, including booking channel, ancillary service selection, and customer travel behaviour. The findings provide actionable insights that can support conversion optimisation and revenue growth strategies.
